# A Comprehensive Analysis of Education in West Bengal in 1994–95

A statistics research paper analyzing the historical evolution, literacy trends, and institutional growth of school and college education in West Bengal, using descriptive statistics on secondary data (Census of India, state education directorates, and Calcutta University records).

**Authors:** Sourav Dey & Roshni Majhi
**Program:** B.Sc. Statistics, UG-2, Sem-4 · Department of Statistics, Presidency University, Kolkata, West Bengal, India

## Overview

This report traces the historical roots, policy milestones, and socio-economic/cultural factors shaping education in West Bengal, using a mixed-methods design built on secondary data and descriptive statistics.

**Objectives:**
- Historical exploration of school education from the colonial period onward
- Policy analysis of key educational reforms and initiatives
- Pedagogical examination of curriculum and teaching methodology changes over time
- Study of societal and cultural influences on educational development

**Key findings from the data:**
- District-wise literacy rates by sex (1981 & 1991 Census)
- Growth in the number of schools (Primary, Middle, High, Higher Secondary) across districts, 1987–1990
- Age-group literacy distribution for Midnapore and Darjeeling districts
- Madhyamik and Higher Secondary examination results (West Bengal Board), 1981–1992
- District-wise number of scholars enrolled, 1987–1990
- Distribution of colleges by type and district (General Degree, B.Ed./Ph.D., Law, Music, Art, Engineering, Technical)
- Calcutta University examination results (B.A., B.Sc., B.Com.), 1985–1990
